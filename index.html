1978 Pocket Simon Game

Basic Front-End Developement Project of the FreeCodeCamp Course.
  • User Story: I am presented with a random series of button presses.
  • User Story: Each time I input a series of button presses correctly, I see the same series of button presses but with an additional step.
  • User Story: I hear a sound that corresponds to each button both when the series of button presses plays, and when I personally press a button.
  • User Story: If I press the wrong button, I am notified that I have done so, and that series of button presses starts again to remind me of the pattern so I can try again.
  • User Story: I can see how many steps are in the current series of button presses.
  • User Story: If I want to restart, I can hit a button to do so, and the game will return to a single step.
  • User Story: I can play in strict mode where if I get a button press wrong, it notifies me that I have done so, and the game restarts at a new random series of button presses.
  • User Story: I can win the game by getting a series of 20 steps correct. I am notified of my victory, then the game starts over.

How I got here:

The 90's. Big hair, rock&roll and the interwebs... Windows95. HTML2. FreeBSD. Apache. Tools I was into it all, I was the BMX Freestyle, Skateboard, RollerBlader who listened to punk rock, and was in love with computers. My first computer was a Tandy, and it didnt stop there, I would take handme downs from wherever I could using 3.1 and eventually FreeBSD. Once Win95 hit and we started learning about HTML2. It was an obsession for my friend and I, multiple pc's lined up on handmade desks running our own apache servers, learning everything we could.

My thought process as I got older was I want to always love doing this so I don't want to to it for a living. That is when I started schooling to become an automotive tech, like many in my family. This left the door open to keep computers a hobby and fun. Over the course of 17 years of being many things, I have always kept computers my top hobby. Either gaming, simple web development, or just building them for friends / family or myself. Web Development has always been something I have enjoyed and held onto. I just only recently decided to make it a career focus, something I wish I did many years ago.

As an automotive tech I learned a lot of things that cross over to Web Development. Like you will never know everything that is why they make books and now databases of knowledge about everything. One of the best quotes one of my teachers told me in my first automotive school was

"Your job is to not remember every make, model, type, your not hired to remember everything, your hired to diagnoise and repair. Your job is to know how and where to look up what is needed for the job your working on."

That has resinated with me over the years in everything I do. I know I will never know everything, there will always be someone smarter, better and faster then me, but that is what drives me. To learn more, to become better, faster and smarter then myself the day before.

In 2008 I injuried my back and am unable to do that type of work anymore.

In 2009 my wife and I started a company called "Vitality Handmade Designs", small company out of the house doing small events. I fell in love with a new hobby Chainmaille. I started designing, creating, and making chainmaille jewlery and accessories. In 2014 we launched a very successful Kickstarter for chainmaille dicebags. I managed over 200 clients, shipping, and creating over 375 bags over the course of 8 months.

2010. Birth of our daughter. I have been a stay at home father since 2010 to my daughter and now my son as well and enjoyed every minute of it so far, but now it's time for a fresh start.

My fiance and I decided in 2015 we were going to move to Arizona and get a fresh start after our son was born in Novemeber. Well plans always need to be altered, our son was born with a heart condition and needed surgery on the east coast (long long story short he is doing just great!). 2016 was a rollercoaster of a time since both my fiance and I decided to start learning new careers (prior to knowing about the surgery). She signed up for college on-line and I was deep into learning Web Development. I still remember sitting bedside at the hospital on the old old laptop trying to wrap my brain around Bootstrap! It was rough to say the least, my fiance worked full-time came home and did her school thing at night, I was taking care of our children from 6am - 8pm then I would sit down and work on learning until 12-1am. Rince and Repeat.. I'm not sharing this story to inact sympothy, or how much of a struggle I went through. I'm sharing in hopes of showing you my dedication. If your looking at portfolio looking for a developer who is dedicated, and has a desire to grow and learn, I am that person. I am humble in my knowledge, that I don't know everything, and I love to share my knowledge when I can. During 2016 I started a online study group on google, hosting weekly hangouts at night. My goal was to reaffirm my knowledge and share that with those who needed help. For those that were in the group seemed to benifit, just as much as I did.
